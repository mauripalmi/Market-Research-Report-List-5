<p><ul><li>Magnetic Levitation Molecular Pump</li><li>Oil Lubricated Molecular Pump</li><li>Grease Lubricated Molecular Pump</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Molecular pumps are crucial for maintaining ultra-high vacuum conditions in semiconductor device manufacturing. The magnetic levitation molecular pump utilizes magnetic bearings for frictionless operation, enhancing efficiency and reducing maintenance. In contrast, oil lubricated molecular pumps use oil to minimize wear and tear, providing reliable performance but may introduce contaminants. Grease lubricated molecular pumps are simpler and cost-effective, using grease for lubrication but requiring more frequent maintenance. Each type serves specific applications based on performance, precision, and operational requirements in semiconductor processes.</p></p>
