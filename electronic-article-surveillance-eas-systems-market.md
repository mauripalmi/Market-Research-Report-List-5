<p><p>Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS) Systems are security tools used predominantly in retail environments to prevent theft and manage inventory. These systems typically consist of tags or labels affixed to merchandise, detection antennas at exits, and deactivation devices at point-of-sale locations. When items pass through the detection systems without being properly deactivated, an alarm is triggered, alerting store personnel to potential theft.</p><p>The Electronic Article Surveillance (EAS) Systems Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.3% during the forecast period, driven by increasing retail theft rates, the need for improved inventory management, and advancements in EAS technologies. Integration with IoT and RFID technologies is becoming increasingly common, enhancing the functionality and efficiency of EAS systems. Retailers are also moving towards more sophisticated systems that offer real-time analytics and monitoring capabilities. Furthermore, the rise of e-commerce has led to the need for innovative loss prevention strategies, further propelling market growth. As consumer behavior evolves, the demand for more robust security solutions in various sectors, including clothing, electronics, and pharmaceuticals, continues to expand, positioning EAS systems as a critical component of retail operations and asset protection strategies.</p></p>
