Market types include Listed Open-ended Funds (LOF), which trade on exchanges like stocks; Contractual Open-ended Funds, with predefined terms and investor contributions; and Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs), which track indexes and are traded throughout the day. Each type offers unique liquidity and investment structures, catering to diverse investor needs.</p></p>

<p><strong>The Open-ended Funds (OEF) Market Industry Research by Application is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Primary and Secondary Markets</li><li>Futures and Spot</li><li>Pegging Index</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Open-ended funds (OEFs) operate in both primary and secondary markets. In the primary market, new shares are created to meet investor demand, allowing direct investment. In the secondary market, existing shares are bought and sold among investors. OEFs can involve futures and spot transactions, where futures contracts allow for future buying/selling at predetermined prices, while spot transactions occur immediately. Additionally, pegging to an index allows OEFs to stabilize value by aligning their performance with a specific benchmark, enhancing investor confidence.</p></p>
