<p><p>Microcrystalline Cellulose (MCC) is a non-toxic, odorless, and tasteless compound widely used in pharmaceuticals, food, and cosmetics. The MCC market can be categorized into two types: wood pulp-based and refined cotton-based. Wood pulp-based MCC is derived from mechanically processed wood fibers, offering cost efficiency and bulk production. In contrast, refined cotton-based MCC is derived from cotton linter, providing higher purity and enhanced functionalities, making it suitable for premium applications in highly regulated sectors like pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
