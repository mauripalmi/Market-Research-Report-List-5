Contact type sensors, such as capacitive and conductive sensors, directly interact with the material, ensuring precise measurement but requiring regular sanitation. Non-contact type sensors, like ultrasonic and radar, measure levels without touching the media, reducing contamination risk and maintenance. Both types cater to strict hygiene standards, with each offering unique advantages in reliability, safety, and ease of integration into clean processes.</p></p>
