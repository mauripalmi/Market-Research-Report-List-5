In contrast, inactive dry yeast is deactivated and primarily used as a nutritional supplement, enriching recipes with vitamins and minerals without causing fermentation. Both types cater to different consumer needs, with active yeast focusing on culinary applications and inactive yeast serving dietary purposes.</p></p>
