<p><p>Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM) is a technology that enables the simultaneous transmission of multiple optical signals over a single fiber by utilizing different wavelengths (colors) of laser light. The market for WDM equipment includes Coarse Wavelength Division Multiplexing (CWDM) and Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing (DWDM). CWDM uses fewer channels spaced apart, making it cost-effective for short-distance applications, while DWDM allows for a higher number of closely spaced channels, catering to long-distance and high-capacity transmission needs.</p></p>
