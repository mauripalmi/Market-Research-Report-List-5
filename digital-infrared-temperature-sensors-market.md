<p><p>Digital infrared temperature sensors are categorized into contact and no-contact types. Contact type sensors measure temperature by direct contact with the object's surface, providing accurate readings in applications like HVAC and industrial processes. No-contact sensors, on the other hand, use infrared technology to gauge the temperature from a distance, making them ideal for situations where contact is impractical, such as in moving machinery or hazardous environments. Both types serve critical roles across various industries, enhancing safety and efficiency.</p></p>
