<p><p>The vaginal panel testing market comprises various methodologies, including Direct Molecular DNA Probe and Molecular Nucleic Acid Amplification Test (NAAT). Direct Molecular DNA Probe involves hybridization techniques to detect specific DNA sequences of pathogens, providing rapid results. In contrast, NAAT amplifies nucleic acids from pathogens, allowing for enhanced sensitivity and specificity in detecting infections. Both methods are crucial in diagnosing sexually transmitted infections and vaginal pathogens, facilitating timely treatment and improving patient outcomes in women's health care.</p></p>
