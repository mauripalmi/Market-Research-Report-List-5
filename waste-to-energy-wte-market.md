<p><p>Waste to Energy (WTE) refers to processes that convert municipal solid waste into usable energy, typically in the form of electricity or heat. The market can be categorized into thermal technologies and biochemical reactions. Thermal technologies involve combustion, gasification, or pyrolysis to transform waste into energy, often producing higher energy yields. In contrast, biochemical processes like anaerobic digestion break down organic materials, generating biogas and compost. Both approaches aim to reduce landfill use and generate sustainable energy while minimizing environmental impact.</p></p>
