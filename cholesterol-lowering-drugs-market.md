<p><p>The cholesterol-lowering drugs market includes several key types: </p><p>1. **Statins**: Widely prescribed to reduce LDL cholesterol by inhibiting its production in the liver. </p><p>2. **Cholesterol Absorption Inhibitors**: These drugs prevent cholesterol absorption in the intestines, aiding in lowering blood cholesterol levels. </p><p>3. **Fibrates**: Primarily used to lower triglycerides and can modestly increase HDL cholesterol. </p><p>4. **PCSK9 Inhibitors**: A newer class, these biologics significantly lower LDL cholesterol by enhancing the liver's ability to remove it from the bloodstream.</p></p>
