<p><p>The seaweed gum market comprises three main types: alginates, agar, and carrageenan. Alginates, derived from brown seaweed, are widely used as thickeners, stabilizers, and gelling agents in food, pharmaceuticals, and textiles. Agar, extracted from red seaweed, is a gelatinous substance used in food, microbiology, and biotechnology for its gelling properties. Carrageenan, also from red seaweed, is a versatile hydrocolloid used as a thickener, stabilizer, and emulsifier in dairy, meat, and personal care products. Each type caters to diverse industries due to their unique functional properties.</p></p>
