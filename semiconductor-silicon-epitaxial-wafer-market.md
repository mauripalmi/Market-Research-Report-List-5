<p><p>The semiconductor silicon epitaxial wafer market is primarily divided into P-type and N-type segments. P-type wafers are doped with elements such as boron, creating "holes" for positive charge carriers, while N-type wafers are doped with elements like phosphorus, allowing excess electrons for negative charge carriers. P-type wafers are commonly used in applications like diodes and transistors, whereas N-type wafers are essential for devices requiring higher electron mobility. The demand for both types is driven by advancements in electronics and renewable energy technologies.</p></p>
