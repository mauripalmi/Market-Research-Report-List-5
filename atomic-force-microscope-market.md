<p><p>An Atomic Force Microscope (AFM) is a type of scanning probe microscope that provides high-resolution imaging at the nanoscale by measuring the forces between a sharp tip and the sample surface. It is widely employed in various fields such as materials science, biology, and nanotechnology for applications like surface topography, mechanical properties analysis, and molecular force measurements. The AFM operates by scanning the tip across the sample surface, utilizing the interaction forces to generate a topographical map with exceptional detail.</p><p>The Atomic Force Microscope Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 1.25% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to the increasing applications of AFM in diverse fields including semiconductor manufacturing, nanotechnology research, and biological studies, driven by the rising demand for precision measurement techniques. Additionally, advancements in AFM technology, such as enhanced imaging speed, automation, and improved sensitivity, are fostering market expansion. Emerging trends, including the integration of AFM with other imaging techniques and the development of portable AFMs, are also shaping the market landscape. As industries prioritize innovation and quality, the demand for Atomic Force Microscopes is likely to continue its upward trajectory.</p></p>
