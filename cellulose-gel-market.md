<p><p>The cellulose gel market is primarily divided into two types: wood pulp-based and refined cotton-based. Wood pulp-based cellulose gel is derived from the fibrous material of trees, offering a cost-effective and abundant source for various applications, including food and pharmaceuticals. Refined cotton-based cellulose gel, on the other hand, is made from high-quality cotton fibers, providing superior purity and gelling properties favored in cosmetics and high-end dietary products. Both types serve distinct market needs, leveraging their unique characteristics.</p></p>
