<p><ul><li>Mediator Microbial Fuel Cell</li><li>Mediator-free Microbial Fuel Cell</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The microbial fuel cell (MFC) market features two main types: mediator and mediator-free MFCs. Mediator MFCs utilize external substances, or mediators, to facilitate electron transfer between microbes and the anode. This can enhance efficiency and performance but may increase costs. Conversely, mediator-free MFCs rely on direct electron transfer from bacteria to the electrode, often resulting in simpler designs and lower operational costs. Both technologies are aimed at sustainable energy generation from organic waste and microbial activity.</p></p>
