<p><p>The Polydioxanone (PDS) suture market is categorized into two main types: monofilament and multifilament. Monofilament sutures consist of a single strand, offering enhanced control and reduced tissue drag, making them ideal for delicate procedures. In contrast, multifilament sutures are composed of several intertwined strands, providing increased strength and flexibility, which is beneficial for securing tissues in more demanding surgical settings. These variations cater to specific surgical needs, influencing choice based on the type of procedure and tissue characteristics.</p></p>
